Jason Reynolds for Senate

Campaign Platform

This is a list of some of the issues that are important to me, my campaign, and my constituents.

The Trump administration continues to show us how fragile our democracy has become.

We must protect the rights and freedoms of all people, regardless of their race, gender identity, sexual orientation, or religion.

As the parent of a trans son, the uncle of mixed-race nieces and nephews, and friend to many non-Christians, I am horrified at the current administration's treatment of minorities and marginalized communities.
When I am elected as your Senator, I pledge to fully support the rights of all races, religions, sexual orientations, and gender identities to freely live their lives to the fullest.

We must enshrine the right for a woman to make their own reproductive healthcare choices.

Roe v. Wade was settled law, until it wasn't. We can no longer trust the courts to rule according to the law and not their personal beliefs. We must enact legislation the guarantees women the right to reproductive healthcare that meets their needs.

Quality, affordable healthcare is a human right.

Healthcare costs are out of control and our profit-first system benefits nobody except the companies and their shareholders. We need to adopt a system where unexpected illnesses don't lead to bankruptcy or force people to skip necessary medical care because of the cost. When I am elected, I will work with my fellow progressives to reform our healthcare system to lower costs.
In addition to reducing costs, we also need to expand access to primary care in rural areas and after hours care in all areas. We need to incentivize medical professionals to serve where the need is the greatest. I believe we can do this by first committing to build hospitals, clinics, and urgent care facilities where we currently have none. To attract the needed staff, we can offer tuition repayment for anyone willing to work at one of the new locations

Millionaires, billionaires, and corporations must be made to pay a fair share.

Trickle down economics have been a complete failure. For decades we have been fed the lie that a lower tax rate on the wealthiest Americans would lead to opportunities for the rest of us. This has led us to the rich continuing to get richer while the poor stay poor. We need to start by eliminating

Current campaign finance rules allow the rich and powerful to buy representation.

The Citizens United decision has been a disaster for America and must be overturned. Corporations and the ultra wealthy have been able to buy unfettered access and influence to politicians on both sides of the aisle resulting in a government run for their benefit and not for the benefit of the ordinary citizen.
To ensure that I am not beholden to corporate or special interests, my campaign will not be accepting funds from corporations or Super PACs. Furthermore, when elected I will not accept meetings or money from lobbyists.

A Cabinet seat should not be a reward for loyalty or campaign donations.'

Cabinet positions should be held by an expert in the field, not the top donors to your election campaign. We need to eliminate the practice of nominating the least qualified but most loyal.
When I am elected not only will I vote 'No' on any Cabinet nominee that is not qualified to serve, I will actively disrupt the process using every procedural tactic possible.

America has become a global outcast.

We were once the leader of the free world, but no longer. We are abandoning our traditional allies and embracing dictators. We are openly encouraging conflict and driving instability. We must renew our partnership with our allies
We need to end the genocide in Gaza immediately by refusing to provide Israel with offensive weaponry. The continued military actions have gone well beyond self defense and are now clearly meant to punish all Palestinians, the majority of which were not eligible to vote in the last parliamentary or presidential elections. Just as we must recognize the right of the Israeli people to exist, we must recognize the right of the Palestinian people to exist and must insist that the two groups begin negotiating for a way to coexist peacefully.
We must also renew our support for Ukraine in their fight against Russian aggression. Allowing Ukraine to fall to the Russians, or negotiating a settlement that does not include the return of all Ukrainian territory is unacceptable. If we allow Russia to conquer Ukraine they will not stop. It will be the first domino on the path to another world war. Despite what Russian apologists will tell you, we are not shipping billions of dollars directly to Ukraine. In fact, around 70% of all aid has actually gone to American companies or to U.S. forces.

Social Security is not a handout and not a privilege.

It is a system that you have paid into every time you've gotten a paycheck.
For years, Congress has been treating Social Security like a a child treats their piggy bank. Constantly borrowing and never paying it back.
It's time that Congress stops borrowing your hard earned money and starts refilling the pot. Most importantly, we need to prohibit the government from stealing our social security funds to pay for other projects. We also need to remove the Social Security tax limit. Why should the wealthy not have to pay Social Security tax on anything earned over $176K. Removing this limit is projected to generate billions of dollars of additional revenue over the next decade.
When elected I will work to introduce legislation to remove the Social Security tax limit and prevent Congress from appropriating our Social Security dollars for any other program.

Anyone working full-time should be able to live off of a single paycheck.

America has ignored its workers for too long. We need to enact a set of progressive policies that ensure working Americans are not left behind again. Minimum wage is too low. Rents are rising too rapidly. Credit card and student loan interest rates are too high. The list goes on and on.
We need legislation to address a minimum wage that has not kept up with rising costs. We need legislation that caps interest rates. We need legislation that encourages states to build more affordable housing. We need legislation that punishes corporations that are guilty of price gouging. These are just some of the issues I will fight for once elected.
